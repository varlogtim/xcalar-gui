Using a published dataflow

This topic describes the tasks you can perform on a published dataflow.

IMPORTANT: A published dataflow is shared among all users on the cluster. If another user changes or deletes a published dataflow when you are using it, you see an alert message. If you want to keep the published dataflow without the other user's changes, copy the published dataflow currently in your dataflow tab, paste it to a new dataflow tab, and save it under a new name.

Viewing a published dataflow

To view a published dataflow, follow these steps:

  1. Click the Dataflow icon to open the Dataflows panel.
  2. Click the folder named Published in the Dataflows panel. The contents of the Published folder are displayed.
  3. Click the published dataflow in the Dataflows panel to display the dataflow in the dataflow editor.

Copying a published dataflow

  1. Display a published dataflow as described in Viewing a published dataflow.
  2. Right click anywhere in the tab displaying the published dataflow to display a menu.
  3. Click Select All in the menu.
  4. Use the keyboard combination (for example, Ctrl-C) to copy the selection.

Deleting a published dataflow

You can delete a published dataflow regardless of whether you are the creator of the dataflow. Also, you can delete a published dataflow when another user is using it.

NOTE: Deletion of a published dataflow cannot be undone.

To delete a published dataflow, follow these steps:

  1. Click the Dataflow icon to open the Dataflows panel.
  2. Navigate to the Published folder in the Dataflows panel.
  3. Locate the published dataflow and then click the trashcan icon. A confirmation message is displayed.
  4. Click Confirm. The published dataflow is removed from the Published folder.

Duplicating a published dataflow

Duplicating a published dataflow requires the same steps as duplicating a dataflow, as described in Duplicating a dataflow

NOTE: The resultant dataflow is created in the Home folder. It is not a published dataflow.

Downloading a published dataflow

Downloading a published dataflow requires the same steps as downloading a dataflow, as described in Downloading and uploading a dataflow.

NOTE: When you upload a dataflow that was downloaded from the Published folder, it is not automatically uploaded to the Published folder. You can specify whether or not the uploaded dataflow is published.

Configuring, executing, and resetting Nodes in a published dataflow

You can configure the Nodes in a published dataflow, execute them, or reset them, in the same way as you do in any dataflow.

Executing a published dataflow

You can execute a published dataflow with or without optimization in the same way as you do in any dataflow.

NOTE: If you do not have the dataset used in the published dataflow, and the user who published the dataflow did not share the dataset, your attempt to execute the published dataflow fails.
